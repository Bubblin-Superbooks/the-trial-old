K. knew very well that this room had recently been let to a typist called 'Miss Buerstner'. She was in the habit of going out to work very early and coming back home very late, and K. had never exchanged more than a few words of greeting with her. Now, her bedside table had been pulled into the middle of the room to be used as a desk for these proceedings, and the supervisor sat behind it. He had his legs crossed, and had thrown one arm over the backrest of the chair.

In one corner of the room there were three young people looking at the photographs belonging to Miss Buerstner that had been put into a piece of fabric on the wall. Hung up on the handle of the open window was a white blouse.
